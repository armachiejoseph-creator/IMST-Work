# Africa CDC-WHO IMST \| User Manual

Welcome to the **Results Framework Matrix** portal. This application is
designed to provide a centralized registry for tracking and managing
epidemiological indicators and public health activities.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

📋 1. Getting Started To begin, navigate to the **Gallery Registry**
tab. This page displays all current indicators stored in the master
database.

### Filtering and Searching

-   **By Level:** Filter results by *Outcome*, *Output*, or *Long-term
    Outcome*.
-   **By Pillar:** Narrow your view to specific technical areas (e.g.,
    Surveillance, Laboratory, RCCE).
-   **Search Bar:** Type keywords to find a specific Indicator ID or
    Name instantly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

✏️ 2. Editing Indicators To update information, follow these steps:

1.  Click the **Edit Details** button on the bottom of any card in the
    Gallery.
2.  You will be automatically redirected to the **Editor** tab.
3.  Modify the necessary fields. If a field is empty in the database, it
    will appear as a blank space.
4.  **History Tracking:** Click the "History" link next to any field
    label to see a log of previous changes, including who made the
    change and when.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

🔐 3. Saving and Audit Logs To maintain data integrity, all changes are
strictly monitored.

-   **Authorized Email:** You must enter your official email address in
    the "Authorized Email" field before saving.
-   **Change Detection:** The system compares your entries against the
    current database. If no actual changes are detected, the system will
    not perform a save.
-   **Audit Sheet:** Every individual field change is recorded in a
    separate `audit_log` sheet, preserving the `old_value` and the
    `new_value`.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

🔄 4. Data Synchronization The application syncs in real-time with
Google Sheets. If you believe the data is out of date due to external
edits: - Click the **Refresh Data** button located in the Filter sidebar
of the Gallery.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## framework for the continental coordination mechanism

**Development of the Results Framework**

The results framework for the Continental Response mechanism was
developed through a structured, pillar-level inquiry using three core
questions for each pillar and one core question for the IMST. This
approach ensures that every pillar within the IMST clearly articulated
its contribution to the overall goal of controlling the outbreak.

**Q0: Why is the IMST established?** This continental coordination
mechanisms are time bound in terms of its operational framework and are
activated to achieve specific results. The main aim is usually  the
long-term impact of supporting Member States to reduce transmission,
morbidity and mortality.

**consultations with each pillar:**

**Q1: Why was the pillar added to the IMST?** This clarified the
specific *purpose* of the pillar (e.g., ensuring timely surveillance,
effective case management, optimal vaccine deployment). The answers to
Q1 were converted into pillar-level outcome statements with its
associated results matrix.

**Q2: What needs to happen in countries to achieve the pillar’s
purpose?** These responses generated the list of “What Must Happen”
(WMH) at country and continental level: high-level results that may
occur with or without the IMST but are essential for outbreak control.
These WMH statements became the country-level outcomes that the IMST
supports indirectly or indirectly.

**Q3: What must the IMST do to make these WMH possible?** This
identified the concrete actions, technical support, strategic guidance,
and operational inputs that each pillar must deliver. These were
converted into IMST output statements, representing the direct products
and services delivered by the IMST (e.g., deployment of experts,
development of guidelines, training, and digital system deployment).
Countries can use Q3 to map the needed input and outputs at the country
level needed to respond to and control the cholera outbreak.

Combining the results across pillars created a unified input-to-impact
results chain that links IMST capacity → IMST actions (outputs) →
country-level results (outcomes) → reduction of cholera transmission and
mortality (impact).

### 1. Surveillance, Early Warning, and Preparedness – Results Framework

| Level | Result Statement |
|:-----------------------------------|:-----------------------------------|
| **Tier 1 Outcome** | Event outbreaks are detected early, accurately, and consistently across affected and at-risk Member States, enabling timely and coordinated response actions. |
| **Tier 2 Outcome 1.1** | Suspected cases and outbreaks are identified, verified, and notified rapidly at national and sub-national levels. |
| **Outputs area contributing to Outcome 1.1** | • Standardized cholera case definitions, alert thresholds, and reporting protocols are applied across Member States.<br>• Event-based and indicator-based surveillance systems are functional at community, facility, and Points of Entry levels.<br>• Surveillance personnel are trained and supported to detect and report suspected cases promptly. |
| **Tier 2 Outcome 1.2** | Surveillance data are complete, accurate, and analyzed in near real-time to inform risk assessment and operational decision-making. (Digitization) |
| **Outputs contributing to Outcome 1.2** | • Routine and outbreak surveillance data are reported consistently through agreed national and regional platforms.<br>• Data validation, analysis, and visualization products are generated regularly to support response planning.<br>• Epidemiological risk assessments and analytical products are produced and disseminated during outbreaks. |
| **Tier 2 Outcome 1.3** | Surveillance information is shared and interpreted across borders to support regional risk monitoring and coordinated preparedness. |
| **Outputs contributing to Outcome 1.3** | • Functional mechanisms for cross-border surveillance data sharing are operational and used.<br>• Regional and continental surveillance dashboards and situation reports are maintained and updated.<br>• Joint risk analyses are conducted for cross-border and multi-country transmission corridors. |
| **IMST Contribution (Enabling Role)** | The IMST supports these results through coordination, harmonization of surveillance tools and protocols, facilitation of cross-border data sharing, provision of surge analytical support, and alignment of national, regional, and continental surveillance efforts. Deployment of surge capacity in CHW’s epidemiologist, etc. |
